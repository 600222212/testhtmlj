General Overview

What CCD is

Colony Collapse disorder is a major problem where many of the working bees in a hive just simply disappear with little trace and leave the queen, lots of food, and nursing bees to care for the young. This means that the hive will die because they need the worker bees for everything. This can cause major problems talked about later. These bees are dying and the cause is that they pollinate many of the farms around the world in the most developed countries the bees are kept in hives and transported around the country to pollinate many fields to get the food that you eat every single day in most things that you eat at home. These fields have farmers that do commercial farming so they will want to make as much food as they can and they do this by monocropping and selling to a particular market. They need bees to pollinate the crops but there are few left, so they have to be transported everywhere.
The bees are not having pesticide poising because they are usually piled up if that happens but these are spread out all over. There are many theories relating to the transport of the bees such as the possibility of the introduction of an invasive varroa mite that kills bees, too much change in the environment, too much change in locations, stress from transport, overstimulation from pesticides, poor nutrition, and probably a combination from above.

Problem Solving

Many countries recognize this threat such as the USA which in 2007 the US department of Agriculture made a CCD steering committe which has made a 4 part action plan

  1. Collect data to determine extent of CCD and also the health of the current honey bee hives.

  2. analysis of bees to determine diseases or pests, stress and immunity, and exposure to pesticides

  3. research whether it is environmental or nutritional stress, pesticides, disease, pests, or a combination of all

  4. prevent further losses and improve bee health and environment.
